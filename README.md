# Bee Hive Defense

## Setup and Interaction Instructions

To run the sketch locally, open `index.html` in Google Chrome using Live Server.

**Controls:**
- Move the cursor using your mouse or trackpad
- Click on animals to hunt them away and protect the bee hives
- Click special ability icons to activate unique abilities
- Restart the game after a game over

Protect the bee hives by hunting away incoming animals before they can damage the hive. Use your special abilities strategically to manage larger groups of enemies and survive for as long as possible.

## Design Rationale

### Affordances

The game uses clear visual distinctions between bee hives, bees, enemies, and ability icons to help players understand how they should interact with each element. Enemy animals are visually different from friendly bees, making it easy for players to recognize threats and respond appropriately. Special ability buttons are designed to appear interactive, encouraging players to click and use them during gameplay.

### Game Flow

The game was designed to balance challenge and player skill. Players begin with simple interactions and gradually face increasing numbers of enemies. As the pace increases, players must react more quickly and use special abilities strategically. This progression helps maintain engagement while providing an appropriate level of challenge.

### Accessibility Considerations

The game uses simple mouse based controls that reduce the complexity of player input. Important objects are visually recognizable and gameplay interactions are designed to be intuitive. The objective remains visible throughout gameplay, helping players stay focused on their primary goal of protecting the hive.

## Iteration Notes

Several improvements were made throughout development based on playtesting feedback. During testing, multiple participants were initially unsure of the game's objective and required time to understand the gameplay loop. The team identified opportunities to improve instructions, onboarding, and communication of player goals.

Playtesting also revealed that some players needed time to become familiar with the controls and game mechanics. However, once players understood the objective, they reported that the gameplay became more engaging and enjoyable. Feedback from participants guided discussions regarding improvements to instructions, enemy behavior, visual communication, and overall gameplay clarity.
